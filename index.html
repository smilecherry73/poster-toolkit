🔍 Reflective Q&A Prompt Bank

☑️ Check questions asked. Use codes (e.g., A1, B3) in notes below to record responses efficiently.

A 🖼️ Visual & Structure

  • A1. If you could keep only one figure, which would it be and why?
  • A2. When viewed as a thumbnail, what information is recognized first?
  • A3. Where does the eye land in the first 5 seconds? Does it match your intention?
  • A4. Is the reading flow (left-to-right, top-to-bottom) intuitive or forced?
  • A5. What visual element could be removed without losing scientific meaning?

B 📖 Scientific Narrative

  • B1. Are you telling a detective story or submitting a lab report?
  • B2. If an editor asked you to highlight a secondary finding, how would you reorganize?
  • B3. Is your conclusion based on data or on interpretation? Is the boundary clear?
  • B4. What is the single most important "take-home message" you want the audience to remember?

C 🗣️ Oral Translation

  • C1. Whose understanding did your preview change? Was that your intended audience?
  • C2. How would you explain the value of this study to a patient's family in one sentence?
  • C3. Did your pace change when explaining complex figures? Intentional or nerves?
  • C4. Did you use any signposting language (e.g., "The key finding here is...")?

D 🤝 Engagement & Adaptability

  • D1. Did any question make you feel challenged? How did you respond internally?
  • D2. If the audience asked about conclusion B instead of A, what does that reveal?
  • D3. Was there a moment you wanted to interrupt? What does that impulse tell you?
  • D4. How did you handle a question you didn't know the answer to?

E 🎭 Delivery Reflection

  • E1. Where did your gaze predominantly rest? How did the audience react?
  • E2. Did your gestures reinforce the message or distract from it?
  • E3. Which rhetorical device (analogy, repetition, etc.) was most effective?
  • E4. If you watched a silent video of your presentation, what would you notice first?

🧭 Instructor Feedback Entry Points & Operations

☑️ Check strategies you applied during this feedback session.

📱 Concept Correction

Action Photograph the poster with a phone and shrink to screen size to test core visibility.

🏷️ Title Intervention

Contrast Descriptive title vs. conclusion-driven title; rewrite on the spot.

👁️ Visual Clinic

Eye Exam Project the poster; have the back row identify the smallest font size.

🎙️ Oral Polishing

Drill Point to the figure with the laser pointer while simultaneously explaining its meaning.

🛡️ Defensive Responding

Phrasing "If I understand correctly, you are asking..." — model academic dialogue.

🎭 Delivery Intervention

Eye Contact Assign three audience members in different zones.
Body Record video; review with sound off.
